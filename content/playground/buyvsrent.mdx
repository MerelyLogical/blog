## Formulae

Renting:

$$
C_{t+1}=(C_t + I - R_t)\cdot(1+r_m)
$$

$$
r_m=(1+r_y)^{\frac{1}{12}}-1
$$

$$
g_m=(1+g_y)^{\frac{1}{12}}-1
$$

$$
R_{t+1}=R_t\cdot(1+g_m)
$$

Buying:

$$
H_0=H
$$

$$
M_0=H_0-D
$$

$$
X_0=C_0-D-B
$$

$$
N=12Y
$$

$$
P=\frac{M_0\cdot m_m(1+m_m)^N}{(1+m_m)^N-1}
$$

If $m_m=0$, then $P=\frac{M_0}{N}$.

$$
M_{t+1}=M_t\cdot(1+m_m)-P_t
$$

$$
O_t=
\frac{o_y}{100}\cdot\frac{H_t}{12}
$$

$$
X_{t+1}=(X_t+I-P_t-O_t)\cdot(1+r_m)
$$

Here $P_t=P$ for most mortgage months, the final payment clears remaining balance, and $P_t=0$ after payoff.

$$
h_m=(1+h_y)^{\frac{1}{12}}-1
$$

$$
H_{t+1}=H_t\cdot(1+h_m)
$$

$$
NW_t=H_t-M_t+X_t
$$

$$
m_m=(1+m_y)^{\frac{1}{12}}-1
$$

Legend: $C$ cash, $C_0$ starting cash, $I$ monthly income, $R_t$ monthly rent at month $t$, $g_y$ annual rent increase rate, $D$ deposit, $B$ one-time buying cost, $H_t$ home value at month $t$, $h_y$ annual home appreciation rate, $M$ mortgage balance, $X$ invested cash, $o_y$ annual ownership cost as a percentage of home value, $r_y$ annual investment return rate, $m_y$ annual mortgage rate, $O_t$ ownership cost in month $t$, $Y$ mortgage term in years, $N$ mortgage term in months, $P$ scheduled monthly mortgage payment, $P_t$ actual payment in month $t$.
